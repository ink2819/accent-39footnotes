January hangs only the droplets of "doing January"[1] on my wall. January slipped away, bringing cold messages and carrying off snake wishes. Thanks for asking.[2]

    Before.  I were a mountain of cobblestones 
chasing the steel-glazed conveyor.
            Do you know.  clay can be soft. the roads are made of waves. 
I and I... lying around for lost flowers. a day has passed. kissing the shoe-sole shape sky
       Before all the before.   a pedestrian was not a pedestrian’s walkway  
           & I was not a stone.  before a destroyed mountain.[3]

Language offers protective shelter,[5]

We are (not) shop owners

Mother tongue is remnants. Rearranged in a structure.[22]

"i" have a carrier bag (the one from Le Guin, you know?). One day, "i" received from Dad a container, the container, and a sequence of more containers—first was the unrecognizable "i" contained in stripes—then in photos—in pencil drawings of the photo and its dated caption lines—in typed texts—in frame...
each container an eye, each "i"/eye seen through your "i"s.[23]

                              Deepseek you raised us with your silver eggs.[25]

AI needs no chopsticks.[26] However, AI is far more fluent than I am in a certain language. I tried several AI tools, comparing them like a savvy shopper, using them as mirrors to reflect the language I struggled with. It just so happens that this language is spoken by the people of my birthplace—my lost mother tongue,[27] drifting like scattered clouds.[28]

We did it: a machine that speaks our languages. But who's their master now?[29]

Who holds all the memories?
Will memories fade like smoke and ashes?                        Perhaps they never truly vanish or blur. Maybe they undergo multiple rounds of archiving and categorization. Maybe they are rearranged by skillful hands, adept in the Korean art of floral arrangement (cocoji, 꽃꽃이), into whimsical shapes—an octopus, a piece of cake, lanterns, a nebula…[30]
